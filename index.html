Our Story

TrowoodCraft specializes in custom-made wooden furniture, blending heritage techniques with modern aesthetics.

Built with Heart. Designed to Last.

Founded in 2010 by master craftsman Thomas Woodrow, TrowoodCraft began as a small workshop dedicated to preserving traditional woodworking techniques while adapting them to contemporary design sensibilities. What started as a passion project has grown into a respected name in artisanal furniture, with our pieces gracing homes, offices, and boutique establishments across the country.

Our philosophy is simple: furniture should be built to last generations, not just seasons. In an era of disposable goods, we stand for honest craftsmanship, durable materials, and tailored design. Each piece we create tells a story - of the wood's origin, the craftsman's skill, and the client's vision coming together to create something truly special.

We carefully select each piece of wood for its unique character and quality. Our artisans then apply time-honored techniques to transform these raw materials into functional works of art. The result is furniture that doesn't just serve a purpose, but enriches your living space with warmth, beauty, and soul.

The Art and Soul of Woodcraft

At TrowoodCraft, we believe that furniture should be more than just functional—it should tell a story, evoke emotion, and stand the test of time. Our journey with each piece begins long before the first cut is made, with a deep respect for the material and the traditions that guide our craft.

The Wood Selection Process

Our commitment to quality starts at the source. We work with sustainable forestry operations and reclaimed wood suppliers to obtain the finest materials. Each plank is carefully inspected for:

  • Grain pattern: We look for interesting, consistent grain that will enhance the finished piece
  • Density and hardness: Ensuring durability appropriate for the furniture's purpose
  • Moisture content: Properly dried wood prevents warping and cracking over time
  • Natural characteristics: Knots, burls, and other features that add character

Traditional Techniques Meet Modern Design

While we honor centuries-old woodworking methods, we're not bound by tradition. Our craftsmen skillfully blend techniques like:

  • Mortise and tenon joinery: Dating back to ancient Egypt, this method creates incredibly strong joints without metal fasteners
  • Dovetail construction: The hallmark of quality drawer construction, cut by hand for precision
  • Steam bending: Allows for graceful curves while maintaining wood strength
  • Hand carving: Adds decorative elements that machines can't replicate

These traditional methods are combined with contemporary design sensibilities to create pieces that feel both timeless and fresh. Our designers stay attuned to modern living needs, creating furniture that fits today's lifestyles while maintaining the quality of heirloom pieces.

The Finishing Touch

Our finishing process is where the wood's true beauty emerges. We use:

  • Natural oil finishes: Penetrate the wood to enhance grain while protecting the surface
  • Low-VOC lacquers: For pieces needing more protection, we use environmentally friendly options
  • Hand-rubbed techniques: Create depth and luster that spray finishes can't match
  • Custom stains: Mixed to achieve exactly the right tone for your space

Each finish is applied by hand, with multiple coats sanded between applications for a silky-smooth surface that feels as good as it looks.

Sustainability at Our Core

We're committed to responsible woodcraft that honors the environment:

  • Sustainably harvested wood: All our materials come from responsibly managed forests or reclaimed sources
  • Zero-waste philosophy: Scraps become smaller items, sawdust is composted, and nothing goes to landfill
  • Non-toxic materials: Safe for your family and our craftsmen
  • Local sourcing: We prioritize domestic woods to reduce transportation impact

This commitment extends beyond materials to how we run our workshop, from energy-efficient lighting to solar panels that power much of our operation.

The Custom Design Experience

Our bespoke service is a collaborative journey:

  1. Initial consultation: We discuss your needs, style preferences, and space requirements
  2. Concept development: Sketches and 3D renderings help visualize the piece
  3. Material selection: Choose from our inventory of premium woods and finishes
  4. Prototype review: For complex pieces, we may create a mockup
  5. Crafting phase: Regular updates with photos of your piece in progress
  6. Final approval: We make any last adjustments before delivery
  7. White-glove delivery: Careful installation in your home

This personalized approach ensures you get exactly what you envision, with the quality only handcrafted furniture can provide.

Caring for Your Investment

Proper care will keep your TrowoodCraft furniture beautiful for generations:

  • Regular dusting: Use a soft, lint-free cloth to prevent buildup
  • Avoid direct sunlight: Can cause fading over time
  • Use coasters and pads: Protect surfaces from heat and moisture
  • Seasonal maintenance: Reapply oil finishes as needed (we provide instructions)
  • Professional restoration: We offer refurbishment services for older pieces

Unlike mass-produced furniture, our pieces can be repaired and refinished as needed, extending their lifespan indefinitely.

Why Handcrafted Matters

In a world of disposable furniture, choosing handcrafted means:

  • Superior durability: Joints that won't loosen, woods that won't warp
  • Unique character: No two pieces are exactly alike
  • Artistic value: Furniture as functional art
  • Environmental benefit: Fewer replacements mean less waste
  • Economic impact: Supporting traditional crafts and local artisans
  • Emotional connection: Pieces with soul that become part of your family story

When you choose TrowoodCraft, you're not just buying furniture—you're investing in a legacy that will outlast trends and temporary styles.
