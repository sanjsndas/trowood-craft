Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our products and services.

How long does a custom furniture piece take to make?

The timeline for custom pieces varies depending on complexity, materials, and our current workshop schedule. Simple pieces may take 4-6 weeks, while more complex projects can take 8-12 weeks or longer. During your initial consultation, we'll provide a more accurate timeline for your specific project.

We never rush the crafting process, as quality is our top priority. Each piece receives the time and attention it needs to meet our exacting standards.

What types of wood do you work with?

We work with a variety of premium hardwoods, including:

  • Oak (white and red)
  • Walnut (American and black)
  • Cherry
  • Maple (hard and soft)
  • Mahogany
  • Teak
  • Sheesham (Indian rosewood)
  • Reclaimed woods from various sources

We're happy to source specific woods upon request, though availability and pricing may vary.

Do you offer delivery and installation?

Yes, we offer white-glove delivery and installation services for all our furniture. Our team will carefully deliver your piece, place it in your desired location, and ensure everything is perfect before leaving.

Local delivery (within 50 miles of our workshop) starts at $150. For deliveries beyond this range, we'll provide a custom quote based on distance and the size of your piece.

Can I visit your workshop?

Absolutely! We welcome visitors by appointment Monday through Friday between 10am and 3pm. Visiting our workshop gives you the opportunity to:

  • See our craftsmen at work
  • View samples of our materials and finishes
  • Discuss custom projects in person
  • Get inspiration from pieces in progress

Please contact us to schedule your visit at least 48 hours in advance.

What is your warranty policy?

All TrowoodCraft furniture comes with a 5-year warranty against defects in materials and workmanship. This covers issues like:

  • Structural failures (joints coming apart, etc.)
  • Warping or cracking under normal use conditions
  • Finish defects (peeling, bubbling, etc.)

The warranty does not cover damage from misuse, accidents, or normal wear and tear. We also offer lifetime support for all our pieces - even after the warranty period, we're happy to advise on care or provide repair services (fees may apply for out-of-warranty work).
